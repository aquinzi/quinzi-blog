Japanese Scrabble

Posted
2012-01-10
Source
Get source

Just out of curiosity, after watching some people play Scrabble, I wanted to know if that game exists for other languages that don't use latin alphabet. As I'm learning Japanese I'll focus on that one.

To tell you the truth, Japanese Scrabble (with kana) doesn't exist. However, there are some variants.

The first that I found is called ロメオ that uses romaji. Therefore, it's a simple adaptation from Scrabble but with "the most used romaji letters". If someone wants it, it costs about 2500円.

Via this site

The other ones I found are called: エンブレインIQプラス あいうえおプラス (with its 2 version), with its "kids" version (box with cute drawings) as well as its "grown up" version (simplier box)... which is the difference? I have no idea beside the boxes. If someone wants to buy it, it cost 1500円.

How the hell do you play that and what about the point system? No idea. It doesn't have a tableu nor the chips have numbers. It must be is a learning game I suppose.... well yeah, obviously.

After a couple of minutes falling to read japanese posts, I realized I could read the game's box. According to this little cute box, and for what I understand, it's something like this:

  • mix the chips and put them on a surface face down
  • take 6
  • take one you don't need out and put it where the rest of the chips are (the ones faced down) but putting it face up, and take another one...
  • rinse and repeat the last step until you have a word/s with those 6 chips? I guess it's allowed to make words with less chips.

But I want to play japanese Scrabble! I know, me too... but it doens't exist per se... the above game only has the 46 kana, no more, no repetition. What you can do is to perform a frequency analysis, create chips according to that, take Scrabble tableu and there you have it! :D Hard and long way, but you have it.
